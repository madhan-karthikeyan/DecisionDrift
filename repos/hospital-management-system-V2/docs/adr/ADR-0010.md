## Context

Patients must pay consultation fees when booking appointments. The payment flow needs to handle order creation, payment verification, and race condition prevention for slot availability.

## Decision

Use Razorpay as the payment gateway. The flow creates a Razorpay order when the patient initiates booking, verifies the payment signature on confirmation, and uses `with_for_update()` row-level locking to prevent double-booking of slots. Payment records are stored in a `PaymentIntent` model with a full audit trail.

## Consequences

Razorpay handles PCI compliance and provides a widely-used payment interface familiar to users. The `with_for_update()` lock prevents race conditions at the database level. The main dependency risk is that Razorpay is a single-vendor integration — switching payment providers would require rewriting the payment endpoints.
